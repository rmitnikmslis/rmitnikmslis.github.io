About Me

I am a student at the Pratt Institute School of Information with interests in Archives, Research Librarianship, and Special Collections. My research interests include [critical] information literacy, critical pedagogy, archives, preserving cultural heritage, affect, addressing library and academic anxiety, organized labor, social reproduction theory, ethics of care, and working with multilingual materials. My passion for libraries is grounded in the library as “the commons” as an ideal in a world where the material reality is that access to information is commodified under neoliberalist capitalism, prioritizing slow and thoughtful engagement and exploration with research inside the classroom, and the joy of lifelong learning in dialogue with meaningful instruction.

I earned a BA in Russian (Language and Literature) from CUNY Hunter College and completed graduate coursework towards an Advanced Certificate in Labor Studies as a Union Semester participant from the CUNY School of Labor and Urban Studies. I was born and raised in deep southern Brooklyn. When I’m not going down a research rabbithole, I am listening and dancing to moody synthesizers, post-punk, disco, klezmer, and “World Music” and finding occasions to hear them live; trying to be an active film viewer at one of this movie town’s great repertory and arthouse cinemas; swimming at Coney Island; and making friends and unsuspecting strangers laugh.

Selected Organizing Experience

    • Developed and maintained databases tracking upcoming real estate development and union market share, including demographic data of Apprentices and Journey Workers in the New York State Department of Labor Apprenticeship Training Program
    • Performed legal and regulatory research to develop analyses of city land-use processes, contractor risk exposure and history, developer and contractor market density, construction funding sources and new policy proposals
    • Interviewed workers and produced written affidavits to document conditions on non-union sites, evaluating possible regulatory violations

  • Housing Works Union - Organizing Committee and Research Subcommittee

    • Identified and developed dozens of committee leaders; recruited and signed up workers for card-check authorization campaign, culminating in majority sign-up of 400 workers
    • Conducted worker outreach across dozens of worksites and job classifications through one-on-ones, group meetings, canvassing, and phone banking
    • Built alliances with local politicians and organizations such as ACT UP New York and Reclaim Pride Coalition for community support
